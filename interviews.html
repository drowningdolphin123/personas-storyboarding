How Hegeman Hall Reacts to the Machines:

  1. How confident do you feel about using the new machines?
    • Most residents interviewed felt confident in using the new machines since they were very similar to previous machines.
    • Some residents interviewed mentioned feeling comfortable in their knowledge of how to do laundry but questioned their confidence because they were nervous about breaking the new machines.
    • The overall consensus is residents felt capabale of using the machines but were wary of accidentally breaking the new machines.

  2. How effective do you think the new stickers are?
    • Some residents interviewed found the new stickers redundant. In particular, the fill line sticker frustrated some residents interviewed because they did not know where the fill line was on the machine.
    • Some residents interviewed were confused by the sticker "No Pods In Tray". They were not sure if this mean no pods at all in the machine, and if so, what type of detergent they were allowed to use.
    • Most residents interviewd thought that, although the stickers were meant to be helpful, they either ignored them or were confused by what the stickers were telling them to do.

  3. Did you encounter any challenges using the new machines?
    • Some residents interviewed felt overwhelmed by the stickers which interfered with their understanding of the new machines.
    • Some residents interviewed realized that they didn't know what the fill line was and were consistently over-filling the machines. This posed an issue because their clothes weren't coming out of the machine fully clean, which led them to believe the new machines were broken.
    • Most residents interviewed mentioned that they did not have too many issues with the user interface of the machines, but were frustrated by their user experience because the previous machines had an app indicating when cycles were done. The new machines don't appear to have one set up yet, and some residents of Hegeman Hall have been disrespectful with other people's laundry.

  4. Have you noticed any changes in the way you do laundry with the new machines?
    • Most residents interviewed didn't notice any changes to their routine for laundry other than an initial quick glance at the new stickers.
    • Some residents interviewed mentioned they were more inclined now to set a timer when doing laundry due to other residents taking over machines who's cycle was done.
    • Overall, residents found their approach to laundry to be the same or slightly changed, and any changes made were because the BrownU App is no longer connected to the washing and drying machines so residents now relied on GroupMe and timers to know if and when machines were open or done.

  5. Would you consider your experience with the new machines positive?
    • Most residents interviewed agreed that their experience with the new machines was positive, and that they preferred the new machines to the old machines.
    • Some residents interviewed expressed that they wished the fill line could be more explicit, especially since there was a note about not filling the machine past the line.
    • All residents interviewed expressed their happiness about the laundry in Hegeman Hall being free for now, but were concerned about what would happen when we would need to pay for laundry again.
